01  /  About Me

Driven by purpose. Grounded in community.

My academic and career goals are clear – I will be a Pediatric Nurse. This journey has been challenging, but I was built for challenges. Born prematurely as a result of an emergency C-section, I went into respiratory distress and suffered from cardiac, pulmonary, and cognitive issues. Airlifted from hospital to hospital at only 4 pounds, I defied the odds that suggested I wouldn't even survive the ride home. That experience — witnessing firsthand the lifesaving impact of compassionate, skilled nursing care — fueled my passion to provide the same hope and healing to other children facing medical crises.

I completed my Associate of Science in Nursing at Miami Dade College's Benjamin Leon School of Nursing in December 2025, and I am currently continuing toward my Bachelor of Science in Nursing, expected April 2027, with a 3.92 GPA. I was honored to receive three full scholarships — the Presidential Scholarship, the American Dream Scholarship, and the DeLuca ADN Nursing Scholarship — reflecting both my academic dedication and my investment in serving others.

My clinical rotations took me across six Miami-Dade healthcare facilities, where I practiced in medical-surgical, psychiatric, rehabilitation, and pediatric settings. These experiences gave me a broad clinical foundation while affirming that pediatric nursing is where I am most called to serve — where sleepless nights and challenging moments are worthwhile because I'm helping children heal.

Before nursing, I invested years in youth-focused work — from tutoring young children as an AmeriCorps Early Learning Tutor to leading enrichment programs through Miami-Dade County Parks and Recreation. Combined with my personal health journey, these experiences have shaped my deep understanding of how dedicated professionals make a profound difference in children's lives, and my commitment to becoming that source of hope, healing, and empathetic care for children in the future.

What I bring to every shift

  • Lived Experience with Medical Resilience

    As a NICU survivor, I understand what families face when their child is critically ill. I bring genuine empathy rooted in personal experience.

  • Therapeutic Communication

    Honed across psychiatric, pediatric, and rehabilitation settings — I meet patients and families where they are, adapting my approach to build trust quickly.

  • Child & Family-Centered Care

    My background in early childhood education and youth programming shapes how I approach pediatric patients — with developmental sensitivity and genuine warmth.

  • Community & Health Equity

    Over 200 volunteer hours across Homestead and Miami underscore my belief that healthcare is a right — and that nurses are uniquely positioned to advance equity.

07  /  Professional Goals

"I entered nursing because I believe every child deserves a nurse who shows up fully — with skill, with presence, and with heart. My goals are built around growing into that nurse, one patient at a time."

Short-Term · Year 1
Complete a Pediatric Nurse Residency

Transition confidently into a structured pediatric residency program, building clinical mastery, deepening my unit-specific skills, and absorbing mentorship from experienced pediatric nurses. My goal is to provide safe, compassionate, independent care to pediatric patients across a range of acuity levels.

Mid-Term · Years 2–3
Complete BSN & Pursue CPN Certification

Finish my BSN at Miami Dade College by April 2027 while practicing as a pediatric RN. Simultaneously accumulate the clinical hours needed to sit for the Certified Pediatric Nurse (CPN) exam through PNCB — a credential that signals deep commitment to excellence in child health.

Mid-Term · Years 2–4
Specialize in Pediatric Oncology or PICU

Deepen my expertise in the pediatric subspecialties where compassionate, highly skilled nursing makes the most profound difference — oncology or critical care. These settings demand everything a nurse has, and I want to be fully prepared to give it.

Long-Term · Years 4–7
Advance into Clinical Education or Leadership

Pursue a charge nurse or clinical educator role — mentoring new graduates the way I hope to be mentored now, and driving evidence-based practice improvements at the unit level. My leadership background in youth programming has prepared me to invest in others' development.

Ongoing
Champion Pediatric Health Equity

Drawing on my roots in Homestead and my community volunteer experience, I plan to advocate within my institution for culturally competent, equitable pediatric care — ensuring that underserved children and families receive the same quality of attention and resources as any other patient.

Ongoing
Stay Grounded in Evidence-Based Practice

Remain engaged with current nursing research, attend professional development opportunities, and contribute to unit-based quality improvement. My 3.92 GPA reflects a genuine love of learning — one I intend to carry into every year of my nursing career.
